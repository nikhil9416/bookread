For most Java applications, we obviously need to ensure that the Singleton works in the presence of multiple threads. But, it looks fairly expensive to synchronize the getInstance() method, so what do we do? Well, we have a few options... 1. Do nothing if the performance of getInstance() isn’t critical to your application That’s right; if calling the getInstance() method isn’t causing substantial overhead for your application, forget about it. Synchronizing getInstance() is straightforward and effective. Just keep in mind that synchronizing a method can decrease performance by a factor of 100, so if a high trafﬁc part of your code begins using getInstance(), you may have to reconsider. 2.
