A: The two are one and the same and you’ll encounter these terms being intermixed. We prefer to use the Principle of Least Knowledge for a couple of reasons: (1) the name is more intuitive and (2) the use of the word “Law” implies we always have to apply this principle. In fact, no principle is a law, all principles should be used when and where they are helpful. All design involves tradeoffs (abstractions versus speed, space versus time, and so on) and while principles provide guidance, all factors should be taken into account before applying them. Q: Are there any disadvantages to applying the Principle of Least Knowledge? A: Yes; while the principle reduces the dependencies between objects and studies have shown this reduces software maintenance, it is also the case that applying this principle results in more “wrapper” classes being written to handle method calls to other components.
