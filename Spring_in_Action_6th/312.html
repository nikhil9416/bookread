285 Getting started with Reactor 11.2.1 Diagramming reactive flows Reactive flows are often illustrated with marble diagrams. Marble diagrams, in their simplest form, depict a timeline of data as it flows through a Flux or Mono at the top, an operation in the middle, and the timeline of the resulting Flux or Mono at the bot- tom. Figure 11.1 shows a marble diagram template for a Flux. As you can see, as data flows through the original Flux, it’s processed through some operation, resulting in a new Flux through which the processed data flows. Figure 11.2 shows a similar marble diagram, but for a Mono. As you can see, the key dif- ference is that a Mono will have either zero or one data item, or an error.
