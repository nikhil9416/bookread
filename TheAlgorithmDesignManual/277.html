A standard cooling schedule was used, with an exponentially decreasing temper- ature (dependent upon the problem size) and a temperature-dependent Boltzmann criterion for accepting states that have higher costs. Our ﬁnal cost function was deﬁned as cost =2 × max + min + (max − min) 2 4 + 4(str total − str in ) where max is the size of the maximum chip dimension, min is the size of the minimum chip dimension, str total = |S|, and str in is the number of strings from S currently on the chip.
