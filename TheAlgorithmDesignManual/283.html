7.11 EXERCISES 271 7-6. [8] In the turnpike reconstruction problem, you are given n(n − 1)/2 distances in sorted order. The problem is to ﬁnd the positions of n points on the line that give rise to these distances. For example, the distances {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6} can be determined by placing the second point 1 unit from the ﬁrst, the third point 3 from the second, and the fourth point 2 from the third. Design and implement an eﬃcient algorithm to report all solutions to the turnpike reconstruction problem. Exploit additive constraints when possible to minimize the search. With proper pruning, problems with hundreds of points can be solved reliably.
