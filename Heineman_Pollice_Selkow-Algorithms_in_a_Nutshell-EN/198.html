AlphaBeta two-ply search AlphaBeta recursively searches through the game tree and maintains two values, α and β, which define a “window of opportunity” for a player as long as α < β. The value α represents the lower bound of the game states found for the player so far (or −∞ if none have been found) and declares that the player has found a move to ensure it can score at least that value. Higher values of α mean the player is doing well; when α = +∞, the player has won and the search can terminate.
