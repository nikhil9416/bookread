14.1 What is Hashing? Hashing is a technique used for storing and retrieving information as quickly as possible. It is used to perform optimal searches and is useful in implementing symbol tables. 14.2 Why Hashing? In the Trees chapter we saw that balanced binary search trees support operations such as insert, delete and search in O(logn) time. In applications, if we need these operations in O(1), then hashing provides a way.
