At the first impression, this solution seems to be having O(n 2 ) complexity. But if we look carefully, every element is pushed and popped at most once, and in every step of the function at least one element is pushed or popped. Since the amount of work for the decisions and the update is constant, the complexity of the algorithm is O(n) by amortized analysis. Space Complexity: O(n) [for stack]. Problem-26 On a given machine, how do you check whether the stack grows up or down? Solution: Try noting down the address of a local variable. Call another function with a local variable declared in it and check the address of that local variable and compare.
