Initially all vertices are marked unvisited (false). The DFS algorithm starts at a vertex u in the graph. By starting at vertex u it considers the edges from u to other vertices. If the edge leads to an already visited vertex, then backtrack to current vertex u. If an edge leads to an unvisited vertex, then go to that vertex and start processing from that vertex. That means the new vertex becomes the current vertex. Follow this process until we reach the dead-end. At this point start backtracking.
