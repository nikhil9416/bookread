• A forest is a disjoint set of trees. • A spanning tree of a connected graph is a subgraph that contains all of that graph’s vertices and is a single tree. Aspanning forest of a graph is the union of spanning trees of its connected components. • A bipartite graph is a graph whose vertices can be divided into two sets such that all edges connect a vertex in one set with a vertex in the other set. • In weighted graphs integers (weights) are assigned to each edge to represent (distances or costs).
