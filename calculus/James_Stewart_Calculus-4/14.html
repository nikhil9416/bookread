1068 CHAPTER 16 Vector Calculus The vectors in Figure 1 are air velocity vectors that indicate the wind speed and direc- tion at points 10 m above the surface elevation in the San Francisco Bay area. We see at a glance from the largest arrows in part (a) that the greatest wind speeds at that time occurred as the winds entered the bay across the Golden Gate Bridge. Part (b) shows the very different wind pattern 12 hours earlier. Associated with every point in the air we can imagine a wind velocity vector. This is an example of a velocity vector ield.
