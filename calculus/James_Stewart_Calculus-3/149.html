(See Sec- tion 3.10.) Here we develop similar ideas in three dimensions. As we zoom in toward a point on a surface that is the graph of a differentiable function of two variables, the sur- face looks more and more like a plane (its tangent plane) and we can approximate the function by a linear function of two variables.
