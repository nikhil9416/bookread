The isothermals are the curves that separate the colored bands. In weather maps of atmospheric pressure at a given time as a function of longitude and latitude, the level curves are called isobars and join locations with the same pres- sure. (See Exercise 34.) Surface winds tend to low from areas of high pressure across the isobars toward areas of low pressure, and are strongest where the isobars are tightly packed. A contour map of world-wide precipitation is shown in Figure 14. Here the level curves are not labeled but they separate the colored regions and the amount of precipita- tion in each region is indicated in the color key.
