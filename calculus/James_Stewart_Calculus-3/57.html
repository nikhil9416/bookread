Quadric Surfaces A quadric surface is the graph of a second-degree equation in three variables x, y, and z. The most general such equation is Ax 2 1 By 2 1 Cz 2 1 Dxy 1 Ey z 1 Fx z 1 Gx 1 Hy 1 I z 1 J - 0 where A, B, C, . . . , J are constants, but by translation and rotation it can be brought into one of the two standard forms Ax 2 1 By 2 1 Cz 2 1 J - 0 or Ax 2 1 By 2 1 I z - 0 Quadric surfaces are the counterparts in three dimensions of the conic sections in the plane. (See Section 10.5 for a review of conic sections.)
