880 CHAPTER 13 Vector Functions Johannes Kepler stated the following three laws of planetary motion on the basis of massive amounts of data on the positions of the planets at various times. Kepler’s Laws 1. A planet revolves around the sun in an elliptical orbit with the sun at one focus. 2. The line joining the sun to a planet sweeps out equal areas in equal times. 3. The square of the period of revolution of a planet is proportional to the cube of the length of the major axis of its orbit. Kepler formulated these laws because they itted the astronomical data. He wasn’t able to see why they were true or how they related to each other. But Sir Isaac Newton, in his Principia Mathematica of 1687, showed how to deduce Kepler’s three laws from two of Newton’s own laws, the Second Law of Motion and the Law of Universal Gravitation. In Section 13.4 we proved Kepler’s First Law using the calculus of vector functions.
