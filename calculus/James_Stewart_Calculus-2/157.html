Cardiac Output Figure 6 shows the human cardiovascular system. Blood returns from the body through the veins, enters the right atrium of the heart, and is pumped to the lungs through the pulmonary arteries for oxygenation. It then lows back into the left atrium through the pulmonary veins and then out to the rest of the body through the aorta. The cardiac output of the heart is the volume of blood pumped by the heart per unit time, that is, the rate of low into the aorta. The dye dilution method is used to measure the cardiac output. Dye is injected into the right atrium and lows through the heart into the aorta.
