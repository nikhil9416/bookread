SECTION 9.3 Separable Equations 603 Orthogonal Trajectories An orthogonal trajectory of a family of curves is a curve that intersects each curve of the family orthogonally, that is, at right angles (see Figure 7). For instance, each mem- ber of the family y - mx of straight lines through the origin is an orthogonal trajectory of the family x 2 1 y 2 - r 2 of concentric circles with center the origin (see Figure 8). We say that the two families are orthogonal trajectories of each other. EXAMPLE 5 Find the orthogonal trajectories of the family of curves x - ky 2 , where k is an arbitrary constant. SOLUTION The curves x - ky 2 form a family of parabolas whose axis of symmetry is the x-axis. The irst step is to ind a single differential equation that is satisied by all members of the family.
