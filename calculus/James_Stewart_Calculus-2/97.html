Computers are particularly good at matching patterns. And just as we used substitutions in conjunction with tables, a CAS can perform substitutions that transform a given integral into one that occurs in its stored formulas. So it isn’t surprising that computer algebra systems excel at integration. That doesn’t mean that integration by hand is an obsolete skill. We will see that a hand computation sometimes produces an indeinite integral in a form that is more convenient than a machine answer.
