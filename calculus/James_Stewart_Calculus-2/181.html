In general, Euler’s method says to start at the point given by the initial value and pro- ceed in the direction indicated by the direction ield. Stop after a short time, look at the slope at the new location, and proceed in that direction. Keep stopping and changing direction according to the direction ield. Euler’s method does not produce the exact solution to an initial-value problem—it gives approximations. But by decreasing the step size (and therefore increasing the number of midcourse corrections), we obtain succes- sively better approximations to the exact solution. (Compare Figures 11, 12, and 13.)
