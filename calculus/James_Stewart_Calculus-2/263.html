If a source of light or sound is placed at one focus of a surface with elliptical cross-sections, then all the light or sound is relected off the surface to the other focus (see Exercise 65). This principle is used in lithotripsy, a treatment for kidney stones. A relector with elliptical cross-section is placed in such a way that the kidney stone is at one focus. High-intensity sound waves generated at the other focus are relected to the stone and destroy it without damaging surrounding tissue. The patient is spared the trauma of surgery and recovers within a few days.
