Anuj, Peggy, om, and Jonny don’t have any neighbors. ey have arrows pointing to them, but no arrows from them to someone else. is is called a directed graph—the relationship is only one way. So Anuj is Bob’s neighbor, but Bob isn’t Anuj’s neighbor. An undirected graph doesn’t have any arrows, and both nodes are each other’s neighbors. For example, both of these graphs are equal.
