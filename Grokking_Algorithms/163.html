144 Chapter 8 I Greedy algorithms A lot of people tell me that this algorithm seems easy. It’s too obvious, so it must be wrong. But that’s the beauty of greedy algorithms: they’re easy! A greedy algorithm is simple: at each step, pick the optimal move. In this case, each time you pick a class, you pick the class that ends the soonest. In technical terms: at each step you pick the locally optimal solution, and in the end you’re leﬅ with the globally optimal solution. Believe it or not, this simple algorithm ﬁnds the optimal solution to this scheduling problem! Obviously, greedy algorithms don’t always work. But they’re simple to write! Let’s look at another example. The knapsack problem Suppose you’re a greedy thief. You’re in a store with a knapsack, and there are all these items you can steal. But you can only take what you can ﬁt in your knapsack. e knapsack can hold 35 pounds. You’re trying to maximize the value of the items you put in your knapsack. What algorithm do you use? Again, the greedy strategy is pretty simple: 1. Pick the most expensive thing that will ﬁt in your knapsack. 2. Pick the next most expensive thing that will ﬁt in your knapsack. And so on.
