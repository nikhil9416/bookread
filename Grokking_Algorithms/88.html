69 Big O notation revisited Notice how you’re not splitting the array into two halves. Instead, one of the sub-arrays is always empty. So the call stack is really long. Now instead, suppose you always picked the middle element as the pivot. Look at the call stack now. It’s so short! Because you divide the array in half every time, you don’t need to make as many recursive calls. You hit the base case sooner, and the call stack is much shorter.
